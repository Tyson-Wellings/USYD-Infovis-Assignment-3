Was Twitter's decision to banish Trump from their platform blatant censorship?

On Jan 6th 2021, supporters of 45th U.S president Donald Trump stormed the U.S Capitol. As members of congress met to recount the electoral votes, an angry mob of Trump supporters battled police officers guarding the Capitol. These police officers were eventually overwhelmed. The mob forcefully made its way into the Capitol causing the congress members to evacuate as they donned gas masks. Eventually the Capitol was cleared. 5 people had lost their lives and 140 were injured. In the aftermath of the events twitter made a decision that some found controversial. On January 8th Twitter decided to officially banish Trump from their platform as they feared his further tweeting could incite more violence.

Trump the Prolific Tweeter

Trump’s term as president began on the the 20th of January 2017 and lasted until the 8th of January 2021, a total of 1449 days had elapsed. During Trump's presidency he had tweeted a total of 16717 times. This means that on average Trump was tweeting 11.53 times per day. However, in reality his use was a lot more sporadic.

It is interesting to note that as the years go by Trump’s twitter usage increases. The largest peak of usage is during October-November which coincided with the commencement of the 2020 presidential election voting. During Trump’s presidency his Twitter following would grow from approximately 20 million followers all the way up to 88 millions followers before he was banned from the platform on the 8th of January 2021. Trump’s frequent use of Twitter helped him build up a cult-like following on the platform. In this article we will examine how Trump’s tweets helped to build a narrative that could eventually incite such extreme mob mentality as evident during the U.S Capitol Riots.

Sticks and Stones may Break my Bones...

Examining 2020-2021 a little closer

So far we have established that Trump tweeted more as his presidency played out and he also began to talk about his opposition more and more. One could argue that this is to be expected from Trump when you consider he would have been going through another presidential campaign. When we analyze all of Trump’s blue tweets from 2020-2021 and create a tally of the vocabulary used we can begin to identify common storylines and tropes. Providing us a more nuanced understanding of the nature of Trump’s name calling and the narratives it creates. In total Trump’s tweets contained 25320 unique words. Let’s draw our attention to the top 253 words, which be the top 0.1% of words tweeted by Trump. Within this top 0.1% we can find the following words: Fake, radical, sleepy, corrupt and lamestream.

When clicking through the different words a few common narratives emerge. The ‘Lamestream’ media is full of fake news. Joe Biden is lazy and unqualified to lead the U.S hence the moniker ‘sleepy’ Joe. The ‘radical’ left controls the ‘Lamestream’ media, social media platforms and the democratic party as a whole. The media and democrats overall are ‘corrupt’. Ironically the fear mongering that Trump claimed the media used against him is actually the second way in which Trump’s tweets contributed to the events of the U.S Capitol Riots.

Putting it all together

Through the exploration of the data presented we can see that Trump in his tweets used name calling, loaded language and repetition to create and reinforce ideas that directly bred distrust in the government (particularly those on the democratic side) and the traditional media. These same ideas created a narrative amongst Trump supporters that America’s only hope for continued prosperity was in Trump repeating as president. It is not surprising that his supporters, once realizing that Trump had lost the election, would behave in a way that as a collective led to the horrific events of the 2021 Capitol riots.
